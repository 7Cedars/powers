# Cultural Stewardship DAO Specification

## Organisational Structure & Context

### *Vision & Mission*

[cite_start]The Cultural Stewardship DAO is a multi-layered ecosystem designed to manage the interplay between physical spaces, ideational concepts, and digital manifestations[cite: 52]. [cite_start]Its primary aim is to act as a steward for cultural assets through a "Layered Approach"[cite: 52], ensuring a clear separation of concerns between the legal entity, the repository, and community engagement.

### *Architecture: Parent & Sub-DAOs*

The organisation operates through a **Parent Organisation** and three distinct types of **Sub-DAOs**:

1.  **Parent Organisation**: The central governance body holding the Treasury (Safe). It manages permissions and funding for the ecosystem but does not act as a sub-DAO itself.
2.  **Sub-DAO #1 (Physical)**: Manages physical manifestations (e.g., access to spaces, rent, legal logs).
3.  **Sub-DAO #2 (Ideational)**: Manages concepts, digital interfaces, and legislative frameworks.
4.  **Sub-DAO #3 (Digital)**: Manages code repositories, commits, and inter-DAO recognition.

### *Treasury Management*

* **Centralised Treasury**: Funds can only be sent to the Safe owned by the Parent Organisation. Sub-DAOs do not control their own payable addresses.
* **Fund Flow**: Sub-DAOs request funding or allowance. The Parent Organisation processes these requests through a governance flow involving Executive execution and Member vetoes.
* **Role Logic**: To prevent security vulnerabilities from overlapping roles, the ecosystem utilises an **ERC-1155** contract for role and token management.

## Off-chain Operations

### *Dispute Resolution*

Disputes regarding ambiguous mandate conditions or malicious actions by role-holders will be addressed through community discussion in the official communication channels.
Final arbitration lies with the **Admin role** of the Parent Organisation if consensus cannot be reached.

### *Code of Conduct*

All participants are expected to act in good faith to further the mission of the Cultural Stewardship DAO. The ecosystem relies on the harmonic interaction between the physical, ideational, and digital layers; disruption in one layer may affect the others.

### *Communication Channels*

Official proposals, discussions, and announcements take place on the DAO's Discord server and community forum.
Note: Sub-DAOs may maintain their own specific channels for "Physical" (Space logistics), "Ideational" (Brainstorming), and "Digital" (Code reviews).

## Description of Governance

The Cultural Stewardship DAO implements a federated governance model.

* **Remit**: To manage a shared treasury (Parent) while empowering specialised Sub-DAOs to operate with autonomy in their respective domains (Physical, Ideational, Digital).
* **Separation of Powers**:
    * **Financial Control**: Centralised at the Parent level to ensure security.
    * **Operational Control**: Decentralised to Sub-DAOs to ensure agility.
    * **Checks and Balances**: Most Sub-DAO actions (like laws or physical access) are executable by local Conveners but subject to Veto by the Parent Executives.
* **Executive Paths**:
    * **Funding**: Sub-DAOs do not hold funds. They act as "cost centres" that request payment execution from the Parent.
    * **Legislation**: Sub-DAOs can create their own internal laws and roles, provided they are not vetoed by the Parent DAO.
* **Summary**: This structure allows for a "Physical manifestation DAO" to worry about rent and keys, while a "Digital manifestation DAO" worries about commits and code, all bound by a common economic and constitutional framework.

## Risk Assessment

### Role Collision
Because roles can be dynamically assigned by Sub-DAOs, there is a risk of overlapping roles creating security vulnerabilities. The system mitigates this by using an **ERC-1155 contract** to ensure unique identification across the ecosystem.

### Dependency Chains
The "Digital DAO" (#3) relies on the recognition of Sibling DAOs (#1 & #2) to execute payments. If recognition logic fails or desynchronises, operations may stall.
